About Sainik School

Sainik Schools are a network of premier educational institutions in India, established with the primary aim of preparing young boys and girls for entry into the National Defence Academy (NDA) and other prestigious military institutions. These schools are managed by the Sainik Schools Society under the Ministry of Defence, Government of India. The first Sainik School was established in 1961 in Satara, Maharashtra, and today, there are over 33 Sainik Schools spread across the country.

Eligibility for Class VI
  1. Age: 10 to 12 years as of 31st March of the admission year.
  2. Qualification: Passed Class V.
Eligibility for Class IX
  1. Age: 13 to 15 years as of 31st March of the admission year.
  2. Qualification: Passed Class VIII.
Application Form
  1. Available on the official Sainik School website.
  2. Typically available from October-November.
Entrance Examination (AISSEE)
  1. Date: Usually held in January.
  2. Class VI: Mathematics, General Knowledge, Language, Intelligence.
  3. Class IX: Mathematics, English, Science, Social Science, Intelligence. – Mode: Pen-and-paper based test.
  4. Duration: 150 minutes (Class VI), 180 minutes (Class IX).
Medical Examination
  1. Conducted for selected candidates.
  2. Assess physical and mental fitness for a military career.
Final Merit List
  1. Based on AISSEE results and medical examination.
